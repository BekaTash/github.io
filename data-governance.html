Benefits of Robust Data Governance

Regulatory Compliance

Meet and exceed regulatory requirements with documented processes, clear data lineage, and comprehensive controls that satisfy auditor scrutiny and reduce compliance risk.

Trusted Analytics

Build confidence in your analytics and reporting by ensuring they're based on high-quality, well-governed data with clear lineage and documented transformations.

Cost Reduction

Reduce costs associated with poor data quality, including rework, manual reconciliation, and inefficient processes. Well-governed data leads to operational efficiency.

Enhanced Collaboration

Break down silos with shared data definitions, clear ownership, and cross-functional governance processes that align business and technical stakeholders.

Improved Data Security

Strengthen data protection with clear classification, access controls, and security policies that safeguard sensitive information and reduce breach risk.

Strategic Decision Support

Enable better strategic decisions with reliable, consistent data that provides a single source of truth across the organization.

Featured Governance Project

BCBS239 Compliance Program for Major Bank

Challenge:

A major New Zealand bank was struggling to meet BCBS239 regulatory requirements for risk data aggregation and reporting. They faced significant challenges with data lineage documentation, data quality controls, and governance processes across their risk reporting ecosystem.

Solution:

I led the implementation of a comprehensive data governance program that included:

  • Development of a tailored BCBS239 compliance framework
  • Implementation of end-to-end data lineage for critical risk reports
  • Establishment of data quality rules and monitoring processes
  • Creation of a governance operating model with clear roles and responsibilities
  • Implementation of metadata management and business glossary

Results:

  • Achieved full compliance with BCBS239 principles within 12 months
  • Reduced data quality issues in risk reporting by 78%
  • Decreased time to respond to regulatory inquiries by 65%
  • Improved confidence in risk reporting among executive leadership
  • Established sustainable governance processes that continue to evolve
